# Vision and Objectives

## Current Research Motto
*Architecture from First Principles.*

## Mission
To discover and define the fundamental computational primitives that enable adaptive cognition, moving beyond monolithic, language-centric AI models.

## Vision
A future where artificial intelligence operates through dynamic, structurally diverse computational mechanisms that adapt automatically to the specific nature of any given problem, mirroring the versatility of true intelligence.

## Current Objective
To rigorously investigate and establish a framework where intelligence is implemented through multiple fundamentally different computational mechanisms dynamically selected according to the nature of the problem.

## Long-Term Objective
To engineer a functional architecture based on these discovered primitives, proving that adaptive computation yields superior generalization and reasoning capabilities across diverse domains compared to single-paradigm architectures.

## Success Criteria
* Identification and rigorous definition of distinct computational primitives.
* Successful demonstration of dynamic scheduling between these primitives on a single problem.
* Empirical proof that this adaptive approach outperforms static pipelines on a structurally complex evaluation domain (such as web design).

## Historical Context: Previous Objectives

  * **Why it evolved:** Council critiques and representation research revealed that the difficulties in design generation stemmed from a deeper issue: the reliance on a single computational paradigm for all cognitive tasks. The objective was broadened to address this root issue.
