# Research Context

This repository does **not** exist to build another Large Language Model.

The research originally began with a different objective:

> Invent a fundamentally new foundation model for design generation.

During extensive research, literature review, architectural discussions, and multiple rounds of AI council critiques, the objective evolved.

The current research objective is broader.

The research now investigates:

> **Whether intelligence should be implemented through one universal computational framework, or through multiple fundamentally different computational mechanisms dynamically selected according to the nature of the problem.**

Current frontier AI systems generally perform every task through one dominant computational paradigm (Transformer variants with different routing, attention patterns, retrieval, MoE, etc.).

This research questions that assumption itself.

Instead of asking

"How can we improve the Transformer?"

the research asks

"Should every form of cognition use the same computational process?"

This became the defining research question.

The research is therefore no longer centered around web design generation.

Web design is now considered only one evaluation domain.

The long-term objective is discovering the computational foundations of adaptive intelligence.

# Current Research Philosophy

Current philosophy:

* Intelligence should not be assumed to emerge from one universal computation.
* Different classes of cognition may require different computational primitives.
* Computation itself may need to adapt according to problem structure.
* Language is treated as an interface rather than intelligence itself.
* Architecture must emerge from first principles and evidence rather than copying existing models.

These are philosophical directions, not proven facts.

Clearly distinguish philosophy from validated findings.

# Documentation Rules

Never overwrite history.

Always preserve chronology.

Never remove rejected ideas.

Never rename accepted principles without documenting why.

Never silently change terminology.

Every important decision gets an identifier.

Every hypothesis gets an identifier.

Every architecture revision gets a version.

Separate facts from hypotheses.

Separate hypotheses from opinions.

Separate discussions from conclusions.

Separate conclusions from implementation.

Always maintain cross references.

Avoid duplicated information.

If information appears in multiple files,

maintain one canonical source and reference it elsewhere.

---

# Research Integrity Rules

Do not strengthen weak evidence.

Do not present hypotheses as facts.

Do not invent missing information.

Do not infer conclusions not explicitly accepted.

Whenever uncertainty exists,

document uncertainty.

Scientific honesty is more important than completeness.
