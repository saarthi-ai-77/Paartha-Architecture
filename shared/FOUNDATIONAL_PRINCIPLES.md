# Foundational Principles

P-001
Methodology precedes ontology.

P-002
Higher-level concepts shall not be defined before their dependencies are established.

P-003
Scientific criticism precedes architectural commitment.

P-004
Foundational claims remain provisional until independently challenged.

P-005
Reduction continues until explanatory collapse.

P-006
Research decisions and rejected hypotheses shall remain permanently traceable.

P-007
Normative behavior shall be investigated as a foundational property of intelligent systems, not merely as a post-training modification.

P-008
Architectural capabilities and normative guarantees shall be researched independently before their interaction is studied.

P-009
The objective of ACA is not maximal intelligence alone, but intelligence that remains scientifically understandable, useful, and aligned with its intended normative principles.

P-010
No architectural capability shall be accepted without evaluating its implications for safety, robustness, and human usefulness.
