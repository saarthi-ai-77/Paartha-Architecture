# Research Manifesto

## Why This Research Exists

This research exists to discover the computational foundations of adaptive intelligence. While the field has largely converged on scaling single-paradigm architectures, we exist to challenge the fundamental premise that all forms of cognition are best served by a single, monolithic computational process. 

## Questioning Current AI Architectures

Current frontier AI systems generally perform every task through one dominant computational paradigm (Transformer variants with different routing, attention patterns, retrieval, MoE, etc.). This research questions that assumption itself. Rather than asking "How can we improve the Transformer?", we ask: "Should every form of cognition use the same computational process?" We posit that different classes of cognition may require fundamentally different computational primitives.

## The Motivation Behind Searching for New Computational Foundations

The motivation is rooted in the observation that intelligence in nature and logic often adapts its structural approach based on the problem at hand. Relying on language as the sole proxy for intelligence limits the structural diversity of computation. By seeking new computational foundations, we aim to build an architecture capable of dynamic selection among multiple mechanisms, thereby achieving true adaptive intelligence rather than monolithic pattern matching.

## Long-term Ambitions

Our long-term ambition is to define and build a universal framework where intelligence is implemented through multiple fundamentally different computational mechanisms dynamically selected according to the nature of the problem. We aim to move beyond language-as-intelligence and ground cognition in true adaptive computation.

## Scientific Principles

1. **First Principles Thinking:** Architecture must emerge from fundamental principles and evidence, not by copying existing models.
2. **Clarity and Separation:** Maintain clear separation between vision, philosophy, architecture, hypotheses, and experiments.
3. **Precision:** Prefer precision over persuasion. We do not use hype or marketing language.
4. **Historical Integrity:** Never rewrite history. Archive deprecated ideas; do not delete them.

## Research Ethics

Scientific honesty is more important than completeness. We do not strengthen weak evidence, present hypotheses as facts, or invent missing information. Whenever uncertainty exists, we explicitly document that uncertainty.

## Research Culture

We cultivate a culture of rigorous critique through a hierarchy of specialized domain councils (the Council System). Ideas are heavily scrutinized within their specific domains, challenged, and refined before being accepted by the Main Council. We respect the evolutionary path of our research, honoring previous architectures like CCA v0.1 as vital stepping stones rather than failures.
