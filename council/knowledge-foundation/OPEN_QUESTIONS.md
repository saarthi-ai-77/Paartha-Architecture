- **ID:** KFC-Q-001
- **Question:** What is a computational primitive? (Are concepts like Distinction, Constraint, Relation, Difference, or State genuinely irreducible?)
- **Current Status:** Active Investigation (Primary Bottleneck)
- **Evidence Collected:** The Reductionist collapsed multiple concepts into Distinction and Constraint. The Reviewer challenged whether Constraint can be further reduced to causal structure or state transitions.
- **Next Investigation:** Analyze candidate computational primitives for true irreducibility.

- **ID:** KFC-Q-002
- **Question:** How do we formally operationalize primitives like "Constraint" and "Distinction" to yield testable predictions?
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Evidence Collected:** The Scientific Reviewer rejected the current structural definition of knowledge for lacking mathematical and operational precision.
- **Next Investigation:** Develop formal operational definitions for accepted computational primitives.

- **ID:** KFC-Q-003
- **Question:** What is the exact boundary that separates knowledge constraints from passive physical traces or universal physical laws?
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Evidence Collected:** The Scientific Reviewer identified logical gaps where the current working definition fails to exclude passive physical traces (e.g., a crack in a rock) or degenerate constraints.
- **Next Investigation:** Formulate a rigorous discriminator between informational constraints and purely physical dynamics.


- **ID:** KFC-Q-004
- **Question:** What constitutes a rigorous proof of irreducibility for a computational primitive?
- **Current Status:** Active Investigation (Sprint 3 Completed)
- **Evidence Collected:** The council appeals to irreducibility but has not defined a formal procedure for proving it, rendering claims of primitiveness unscientific.
- **Next Investigation:** Develop a formal methodology for validating irreducibility.

- **ID:** KFC-Q-005
- **Question:** How can the council construct a robust falsification procedure for candidate primitives?
- **Current Status:** Active Investigation (Sprint 3 Completed)
- **Evidence Collected:** The Scientific Reviewer criticized the current framework for lacking a mechanism to mathematically or computationally reject incorrect primitive candidates.
- **Next Investigation:** Build a falsification methodology alongside the proof of irreducibility.

- **ID:** KFC-Q-006
- **Question:** Can the four evaluation criteria for primitiveness be collapsed into just two: Irreducibility and Generativity?
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Evidence Collected:** The Reductionist demonstrated that Irreducibility, Generative Necessity, Explanatory Ineliminability, and Non-Decomposability can plausibly collapse into two orthogonal criteria.
- **Next Investigation:** Verify if Irreducibility and Generativity are jointly necessary and sufficient.


- **ID:** KFC-Q-007
- **Question:** Can the proposed primitive validation methodology (PRIMITIVE) validate itself without circularity?
- **Current Status:** Active Investigation (Sprint 3A Focus)
- **Evidence Collected:** The Scientific Reviewer identified a meta-problem: if the framework evaluates all future primitive claims, it becomes a scientific hypothesis that requires independent validation.
- **Next Investigation:** Address meta-validation and the epistemological foundations of the framework during Sprint 3A.

- **ID:** KFC-Q-008
- **Question:** What constitutes sufficient evidence for provisional acceptance or rejection of a computational primitive?
- **Current Status:** Active Investigation (Sprint 3A Focus)
- **Evidence Collected:** The current methodology provides evaluation stages but lacks explicit decision criteria (e.g., how much failed criticism is enough to accept a primitive).
- **Next Investigation:** Define explicit acceptance, provisional acceptance, and rejection thresholds in Sprint 3A.

- **ID:** KFC-Q-009
- **Question:** How can independent researchers produce reproducible evaluations of primitive candidates?
- **Current Status:** Active Investigation (Sprint 3A Focus)
- **Evidence Collected:** The Scientific Reviewer warned that without operational scoring or decision rules, the framework risks relying on subjective expert judgment.
- **Next Investigation:** Establish formal reproducibility criteria and standardized evaluation rules.
