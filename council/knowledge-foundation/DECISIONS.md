- **ID:** KFC-DEC-001
- **Decision:** The research bottleneck must shift from defining "knowledge" to discovering fundamental "computational primitives".
- **Rationale:** The council realized that any structural definition of knowledge relies on concepts (like Distinction, Constraint, or Relation) whose irreducibility has not been proven. Identifying true computational primitives must precede defining knowledge.
- **Supporting Evidence:** The Reductionist successfully collapsed six proposed primitives into essentially Distinction and Constraint. The Scientific Reviewer subsequently demonstrated that the primitive status of Constraint itself remains unproven.
